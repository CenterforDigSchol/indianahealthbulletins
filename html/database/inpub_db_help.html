Geographical Regions

    All geographical regions are in the state of Indiana.

  • Northern counties:

    Adams, Allen, Benton, Blackford, Carroll, Cass, De Kalb, Elkhart, Fulton, Howard, Huntington, Jasper, Jay, Kosciusko, La Grange, Lake, La Porte, Marshall, Miami, Newton, Noble, Porter, Pulaski, Starke, Steuben, St. Joseph, Wabash, Wells, White, Whitley

  • Central counties:

    Bartholomew, Boone, Brown, Clinton, Decatur, Delaware, Fayette, Fountain, Franklin, Hamilton, Hancock, Hendricks, Henry, Johnson, Madison, Marion, Monroe, Montgomery, Morgan, Owen, Parke, Putnam, Randolph, Ripley, Rush, Shelby, Tippecanoe, Tipton, Union, Vermillion, Vigo, Warren, Wayne

  • Southern counties:

    Clark, Clay, Crawford, Daviess, Dearborn, Dubois, Floyd, Greene, Harrison, Jackson, Jefferson, Jennings, Knox, Lawrence, Martin, Morgan, Ohio, Orange, Perry, Pike, Posey, Scott, Spencer, Sullivan, Switzerland, Vanderburgh, Warrick, Washington

  • Urban geographical region includes all incorporated cities.

  • Rural geographical region includes population outside of incorporated cities.

    Cities:

    During the October 1899 - December 1910 time period, cities were divided into the following five classes, based on population:

    1. Cities with populations over 50,000
    2. Cities with populations from 25,000 to 50,000
    3. Cities with populations from 10,000 to 25,000
    4. Cities with populations from 5,000 to 10,000
    5. Cities with populations under 5,000

    During the January 1911 - March 1923 time period, cities were divided into the following five classes, based on population:

    1. Cities with populations over 100,000
    2. Cities with populations from 45,000 to 100,000
    3. Cities with populations from 20,000 to 45,000
    4. Cities with populations from 10,000 to 20,000
    5. Cities with populations from 5,000 to 10,000

Statistics

    Some diseases were referred to by different names over the years. The following diseases had multiple names:

    • Pulmonary Tuberculosis is also referred to as Pulmonary Consumption.
    • Pneumonia is also referred to as Lobar and Broncho-Pneumonia.
    • Diarrhea and Enteritis, Under 2 Years is also referred to as Diarrheal Diseases. Under Two.
    • Meningitis is used for Cerebro-Spinal Fever, Cerebro-Spinal Meningitis, and Epidemic Meningitis.
    • Poliomyelitis is also referred to as Acute Anterior Poliomyelitis.
    • Puerperal is used for Puerperal Septicemia and Puerperal Causes.
    • Apoplexy is also referred to as Cerebral Hemorrhage.
